WWI PROPAGANDA POSTERS

In order to persuade New Zealand soldiers to enlist during the First World War, the government decided to release recruitment posters as a use of propaganda. These posters mentioned below conveyed many controversial messages to the nation during that time. After looking and reading through the page, have a discussion with your classmates and decide whether or not these posters convey offensive meanings.

“The Empire Needs Men!”

During the First World War, United Kingdom often used animals, such as a lion, in their recruitment posters as it was one of their national symbols. The use of the cubs underneath the lion implicated a parent-child relationship between Britain and other countries who were under their wings at that time such as Australia, Canada, India & New Zealand. The heading 'THE EMPIRE NEEDS MEN!" suggests that men were only needed during the war, which encouraged patriotism in society during that time.

“Women of Britain say - ‘Go!’”

Although this piece was originally produced for Britain, it would be one of the reasons behind why many men soldiers enlisted during the First World War. The use of the women looking longingly at the walking soldiers suggested that women had to support their husbands, brothers or sons who have enlisted for the war. Additionally, the poster also attempts to humiliate and 'pick a dig' at the men who have not yet enlisted and therefore considered 'unsupportive' of their country and their family.

“Follow me - your country NEEDS you!”

Another interesting recruitment poster piece. Using the male soldier as the only subject of the poster signifies that men were the only people "needed" for the war, further encouraging patriotism and sexism that occurred in New Zealand society during that time.
